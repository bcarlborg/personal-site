This is the understanding from the second chapter of Goldrei's book that finally helped things click for me.
I tried to continually refine and add to this list as I worked on the solutions to hone it in more and more.
In a way, it is like my study guide for propositional logic based on chapter 2. 

- interpretations are truth assignments
- those interpretations that make the formula true are models
- a formula is like a set of constraints on the universe of interpretations.
  - the formula splits up the space of models into the ones that are true and the ones that are false
